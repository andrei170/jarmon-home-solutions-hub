The one concrete thing I found

Your structured data only declares a generic "WebSite"

I checked your raw page source. The only schema on the site is the Wix default WebSite type. There is no LocalBusiness, no Electrician, and no Service schema. That is the machine-readable record that tells Google and AI search you are a licensed local electrician who installs EV chargers in named counties. Right now that record does not exist. For a dual-licensed local operator, that is the clearest fixable gap on the site.

It is also the easiest to demonstrate, so I did. I built you a sample EV-charger landing page with the corrected schema baked in, the exact thing your live site is missing, marked in the code.

The three fixes to own "EV charger installation Cincinnati"

  1. Add the missing schemaLocalBusiness / Electrician + Service structured data with your real name, phone, license note, and the five counties you serve. Shown working in the demo.
  2. Optimize the EV page on-pageTarget the actual phrase in the H1, intro, and title, plus an answer-first FAQ (cost, permits, panel load) so Google and AI engines can cite you.
  3. Point a small, focused push at itTighten local signals (GBP, per-county targeting), then a small Google Search / Local Services Ads test on EV terms once the page is ready to receive traffic.
